Commercial station programs are now being judged on transition realism as much as hardware progress. For Axiom, that means demonstrating stepwise integration with ISS-era operations while preparing for independent station phases.

Crew rotation logistics, cargo cadence, and emergency-response procedures remain the core operational milestones. None are glamorous, but all are required for confidence from agencies and private customers.

The strategic objective is continuity in low-Earth-orbit research and manufacturing as ISS winds down. If transition schedules hold, the handoff could become a template for future public-private orbital infrastructure.
