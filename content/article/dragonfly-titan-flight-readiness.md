Dragonfly introduces a different operations profile from traditional landers: repeated flight hops, local science campaigns, and autonomous hazard decisions under communication delay.

Readiness reviews are therefore focused on fault handling and navigation confidence, not only mechanical qualification. Teams are training for off-nominal branches where conservative decisions preserve vehicle health at the cost of immediate science.

If successful, Dragonfly will demonstrate a mobile exploration model suited to atmospheres and complex terrain. Its operational legacy may extend well beyond Titan.
