Perseverance is proving that methodical surface operations can sustain both science return and sample caching pace. Traverses are chosen for geological diversity, but the sequence still reflects strict engineering constraints.

Data relay through orbiters remains a silent dependency. Without reliable communications windows, surface teams cannot maintain efficient tactical planning.

The rover's success is not just in discoveries; it is in demonstrating repeatable workflows for future sample return campaigns.
