Mars Sample Return has shifted from concept excitement to architecture realism. Launch windows, vehicle mass budgets, and risk posture all interact in ways that force tradeoffs between schedule and complexity.

The scientific value remains exceptional: sealed cores from Jezero Crater can answer formation and habitability questions that in-situ instruments cannot fully resolve.

The current debate is less about whether to return samples and more about how to deliver them with acceptable cost and reliability.
