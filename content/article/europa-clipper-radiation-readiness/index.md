Europa Clipper operations planning has focused heavily on survivability in Jupiter's radiation environment. The spacecraft does not orbit Europa directly; instead, it executes repeated flybys that balance science return with hardware lifetime.

Instrument teams are sequencing observations around strict power, thermal, and pointing constraints. Radar and spectroscopy campaigns require tightly coordinated windows, and missed opportunities can be expensive when orbital geometry does not repeat quickly.

Mission managers describe the strategy as a marathon of disciplined flybys rather than a single dramatic event. If timing and health margins hold, Clipper should deliver the most complete pre-lander map of Europa's ice shell and subsurface context.
