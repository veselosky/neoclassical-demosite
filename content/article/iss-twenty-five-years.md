The ISS remains a living laboratory for human factors, life support resilience, and long-duration operations. Crew rotation and cargo planning are now mature, but still sensitive to launch disruptions.

International cooperation is both strength and complexity. Shared infrastructure demands stable coordination at technical and political levels.

As commercial stations emerge, ISS lessons will shape standards for safety, interoperability, and mission governance.
