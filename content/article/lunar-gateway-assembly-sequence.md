Gateway planning has shifted from architecture diagrams to real sequencing questions: which module arrives first, when logistics can be sustained, and how docking schedules absorb launch slips.

Each assembly step changes power, communications, and fault-management assumptions. That forces integration teams to validate software and procedures at each milestone rather than waiting for a fully built configuration.

Program officials continue to frame Gateway as an enabling platform for sustained lunar operations. In operational terms, that means proving incremental reliability under constrained windows, not just completing a checklist of launches.
