Starship discussions often emphasize launch spectacle, but Mars logistics are mostly about operations density: propellant production, cargo sequencing, and failure-tolerant supply chains.

A reusable architecture can lower per-flight costs, yet mission economics still depend on flight rate and reliability trends over years.

The practical test for any Mars transport system is whether it can sustain not one mission, but a repeatable campaign.
