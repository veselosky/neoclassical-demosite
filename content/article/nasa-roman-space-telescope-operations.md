NASA's Roman Space Telescope is being built for wide-field survey throughput, not boutique targeting. That means operations success will be measured in cadence reliability, calibration consistency, and data pipeline resilience over long campaign windows.

Unlike single-object observatories, Roman's value compounds when large sky fractions are processed with uniform quality. Teams are already rehearsing downlink and processing rhythms to avoid bottlenecks once full survey volume arrives.

The practical question for operations is less "can we observe this field" and more "can we sustain this pace for years without quality drift." If Roman delivers on that promise, it will be a cornerstone mission for dark energy and exoplanet demographics.
