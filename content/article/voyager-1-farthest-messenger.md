Voyager 1 crossed into interstellar space in 2012, yet it still returns engineering telemetry through the Deep Space Network. The data stream is thin and fragile, but it is enough to keep refining our understanding of plasma waves and particle density beyond the heliopause.

NASA operations teams now treat every command sequence as a high-stakes negotiation with power limits. Engineers are selectively powering instruments to preserve mission life while still collecting useful science.

Voyager was never designed for this duration, which makes its longevity one of the strongest arguments for conservative systems engineering in planetary missions.
