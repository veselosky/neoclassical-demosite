Solar Orbiter's high-latitude observations are changing how teams think about magnetic field transport from solar poles into the heliosphere. The challenge is coordinating remote sensing with in-situ measurements under strict telemetry limits.

During polar-pass campaigns, instrument timelines become tightly choreographed. Teams prioritize packets that preserve context across multiple instruments instead of maximizing any single channel.

Early products suggest the mission is already delivering a more complete operational picture of solar cycle behavior. The long-term payoff will come from repeating these campaigns across different phases of the cycle.
